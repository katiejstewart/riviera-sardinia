How this trip flows

A slow, spa-forward rhythm across two famous coasts. You fly Miami → Madrid → Milan on Air Europa, then a private transfer from Malpensa down to Santa Margherita Ligure for the Portofino coast and a Cinque Terre day. Then the one flight inside the trip — the unavoidable island hop, Milan → Olbia — drops you into the Costa Smeralda. You finish with an open couple of days based around Milan before the same Madrid connection home.

Riviera

Santa Margherita, Paraggi & Portofino

Sun · Aug 2 · your Italian Riviera day

The postcard day, on foot — a pretty Santa Margherita boutique loop, a beach-club lunch at Paraggi, then Portofino for the small luxury shopping triangle before the ferry back. No churches, no rushed sightseeing; just waterfront, boutiques, lunch, gelato and yachts.

Morning route
Start at the hotel and drift into Santa Margherita by the water: Via Pagana → Lungomare Raffaele Rossetti → Piazza Martiri della Libertà. Then turn into the shopping streets: Via Palestro for the caruggio feel, Via Cavour for Seghezzo gourmet gifts, and Corso Matteotti for an easy waterfront finish.
Shops + sweets
Make this the Ligurian souvenir pass: Seghezzo for pesto, olive oil and pantry gifts; Pasticceria Arte Dolce for margheritine cookies; Fiordiponti if you want focaccia before the walk. Gelato: Gelateria Centrale for the chocolate-dipped Pinguino or Bar Vittoria for panera semifreddo.
Walk
Late morning, follow the seaside road/path toward Paraggi: Lungomare Rossetti → Via Milite Ignoto / SP227, with water on your right and villas above you. It is ~45–60 minutes to the emerald cove; take a taxi instead if it is too hot.
Lunch
12:30 at Langosteria Paraggi / Dior beach club: beautiful setting, fashion beach-club atmosphere, exactly the luxury Riviera vibe. Status (Jul 9): 12:30 table for 2 confirmed available on SevenRooms — needs Katie's credit card (no-show guarantee) to finalize; allergy tags prefill fine. Note: seafood-led kitchen — reconfirm fish-free dishes on arrival. Leave Paraggi around 2:45 PM so Portofino shopping does not get squeezed.
Portofino
Walk or quick taxi to Portofino, then shop the compact triangle: Via Roma → the Piazzetta → Calata Marconi / Molo Umberto I. Look for Loro Piana, Louis Vuitton, Dior if the seasonal pop-up is active, Missoni, Modes, Rocca 1794 for jewelry/watches, and Antica Calzoleria Mingo for the charming local shoe stop. Gelato: Calata 32 on the marina or Gepi near the Piazzetta.
Return
Aim for the 6:00 PM ferry Portofino → Santa Margherita so there is buffer before dinner. Ferry is prettier than taxi and keeps the whole day coastal.
Evening
Dinner at Madachì — 8:30 PM (reserved).
Dining: Lunch Langosteria / Dior Paraggi (12:30 available · finalize with card) · Dinner Madachì (8:30 PM, reserved). Fallback lunch: Lo Stella in Portofino.

Riviera · the must-see

Cinque Terre

Mon · Aug 3 · two–three villages, ending on the water

Quality over quantity — flat, pretty Monterosso as the anchor; Vernazza for the harbour and one perfect postcard street; then the day's big upgrade: a sunset boat cruise watching Cinque Terre glow from the sea. No hiking agenda, no churches, no rushed five-village checklist.

Morning train
Train Santa Margherita → Monterosso (~1 hr). Arrive in Fegina, the flatter beach side, and start easy on Via Fegina: beach promenade, umbrellas, the Giant/Neptune exterior, coffee with a sea view.
Monterosso loop
Walk through the pedestrian tunnel into the old town, then use Via Roma → Piazza Garibaldi → Via Vittorio Emanuele as the wander. This is the best mix of ceramics, linen, pesto shops, wine, tiny boutiques and gelato without stairs. Good stops: Enoteca Internazionale for local wine/pantry gifts, Pesto Lab-style food shops, and Slurp or Il Golosone for gelato.
Lunch
La Cantina di Miky on the Fegina side — the current lunch favorite. Status (Jul 9): requested 12:15, outdoor for 2 via their online form (lunch seatings are 12:00/12:15 only — no 1:00 PM), allergy noted — awaiting their confirmation by email/phone.
Vernazza
After lunch, take the train or ferry to Vernazza. Keep it simple: Via Roma down to Piazza Marconi and the harbour. Grandma can hold a waterfront table while you poke into the tiny shops or take photos. Gelato: Gelateria Vernazza or Il Porticciolo. Skip the castle stairs.
Boat
Return to Monterosso if the boat departs there. ⭐ Sunset boat cruise — comfortable small group, aperitivo aboard, no swim-focused full-day plan. The boat is the headline; the villages are the slow lead-in.
Dinner
Torre Aurora in Monterosso is the selected direction for a nice Cinque Terre dinner after the boat: dramatic setting, sea views, cocktails first, and no long transfer before eating. Quality filter: confirm the kitchen can do a genuinely strong, allergy-safe fish-free dinner; if it feels view-first/food-second, switch to Ristorante Miky or L'Ancora della Tortuga in Monterosso. Tue · Aug 4 · the relaxed morning, then the island hop

The most relaxed, authentic morning of the trip — a guided-feeling self-wander through Camogli's harbour, waterfront, focaccia shops and little streets before the afternoon transfer and the flight to Sardinian granite and emerald water.

Route
Go Santa Margherita → Camogli by train/taxi, then start at Piazza Colombo and the harbour. Walk the flat waterfront along Via Garibaldi, with the tall trompe-l'oeil houses on one side and the sea on the other. Use the ramp/viewpoint by Castello della Dragonara for photos, then loop back toward Via della Repubblica for the small-town shop stretch.

No church interiors, no stairs unless you feel like it.

Sardinia · signature day

La Maddalena archipelago by boat

Thu · Aug 6 · the signature Sardinia day

The Sardinian water headline — La Maddalena by a nice boat, ideally a polished small-group catamaran or motor boat rather than a crowded 100-person ferry. The goal is beautiful water, stable comfort, a good lunch, and Grandma can stay aboard while others swim.

Boat style
Your ideal: a shared catamaran that still feels private — a small group aboard, not a 100-person party ferry. The three picks below all fit that shape; Cannigione is the closest departure to Porto Cervo. Allergy note: the standard shared-boat lunch is fish-based pasta prepared onboard — pre-arrange a fish-free plate at booking, or take the private upgrade (Kiss From Italy) to fully control the menu and let Grandma stay aboard while others swim.
Route
Best route: Spargi → Budelli / Manto della Madonna → Santa Maria → Caprera, with slow passes through turquoise shallows and calm anchorages. If choosing a shorter hotel-partner tour, the 3-hour Caprera by RIB from Poltu Quatu is convenient but less luxurious.
Onboard
Prioritize shade, cushioned seating, restroom if possible, and a real lunch/aperitivo. Swimming is optional; Grandma can stay aboard and enjoy the scenery. Ask explicitly about boarding ease and allergy-safe food.
Schedule flag
Thursday is also San Pantaleo market day, the chic artisan village/market near Costa Smeralda. If the market matters, move the boat to Aug 5 and put beach-club/spa on Aug 6, or do market early only if the boat is an afternoon departure.
Evening
The Sardinian feast — Antica Gallura (Arzachena) for porceddu and Gallura classics, the safest and most authentic dinner for the allergy. Before you go

Know before you go

Timing & events

  • Peak season: early August is the busiest, priciest week on both coasts — book hotels, transfers, the boat and dinners well ahead.
  • Cinque Terre on a Monday (Aug 3) is busy but a touch calmer than the weekend; early train and afternoon ferry is the way.
  • Porto Cervo is at its glittering peak in early August — worth a dinner reservation everywhere.
  • Ferragosto (Aug 15) falls after the trip, so you miss the most chaotic day — but it does thin out Milan's restaurants.

Pacing for two

  • The arrival day is long — a redeye, a Madrid connection, then a drive. Keep the first evening loose.
  • Cinque Terre villages are steep — flat Monterosso plus Vernazza, by train and ferry, skip the trails and the car.
  • Your Grand Hotel is about 50 meters from Cala Granu beach; umbrellas/sunbeds should be reserved ahead.
  • The La Maddalena boat day should be a nice small-group catamaran or crewed motor boat — beautiful, comfortable, not a crowded party ferry.

A note on the fish allergy

  • Both coasts love seafood — flag it every time: "Sono allergica al pesce e ai frutti di mare" (or "grave allergia a pesce e crostacei"). In Liguria, watch for anchovies (acciughe) hidden in sauces.
  • Liguria is easy: pesto (trofie/trenette), focaccia, farinata, pansoti with walnut sauce — all fish-free.
  • Sardinia too: porceddu (roast suckling pig), culurgiones, pane carasau, pecorino, and seadas for dessert.
  • Milan too: cotoletta, risotto alla milanese, ossobuco — all naturally fish-free. Carry an allergy card and meds, especially on boat days.

Getting around

  • Private transfers on the two long Malpensa legs (Aug 1, Aug 4) — restful, no driving or parking.
  • Portofino by ferry from Santa Margherita; local taxis for short hops in town.
  • Cinque Terre by train — far easier than a car; Cinque Terre Express between villages, ferry back.
  • In Sardinia, private transfers and the hotel cover Porto Cervo; add a driver for beach/inland days.

Good to know

  • Italy uses Type F/L plugs, 230V — bring adapters.
  • Carry some cash; smaller spots can be card-shy.
  • Validate regional train tickets before boarding where required.
  • Reconfirm the Milan airport on the Aug 7 return matches the Aug 9 Milan → Madrid departure.
