How this version works

Same slow, spa-forward rhythm as Puglia and Sicily, across two famous coasts. You keep the American round trip into and out of Rome, then instead of a second flight you take a scenic train down to the Riviera — no extra airport on arrival. The only flight inside the trip is the one unavoidable island crossing, Genoa → Olbia, mid-trip.

What to book, in what order

  1. American round trip, Rome.
    Book MIA ⇄ FCO on American (AA052 out Jul 31 / AA053 home Aug 7). Choose Flagship Business for Grandma's lie-flat on the 777 — this is the leg that matters.
  2. The train to Rapallo.
    Roma Termini → Rapallo on Trenitalia (Frecciarossa/Frecciabianca). Book a few weeks out; reserve Business or Executive seats. Plus the Leonardo Express FCO→Termini.
  3. The two island flights.
    Genoa → Olbia on Volotea (confirm Tue Aug 4), and Olbia → Rome on ITA/Volotea for the morning of Aug 7 — both economy, separate from the American ticket.
  4. Transfers & experiences.
    Private cars (Rapallo→Genoa, Olbia→Porto Cervo, Porto Cervo→Olbia), the Portofino ferry, Cinque Terre passes, and the La Maddalena boat. Reserve dinners — peak August.

Contingencies & flags

Confirm the Tuesday Genoa → Olbia flight

Genoa → Olbia runs about 8 times a week in summer — frequent but not daily. Verify a flight on Tue Aug 4 on Volotea or ITA. If the day has none, the clean fallback is Genoa → Rome → Olbia on ITA the same day; an overnight ferry exists but isn't worth it.

The return is on two tickets

American doesn't fly Olbia, so the Olbia → Rome feeder is a separate ticket from the American transatlantic. This plan leaves ~5 hrs in Rome, which is comfortable. For zero risk, fly Olbia → Rome the evening before and overnight at an FCO airport hotel — at the cost of one Sardinia night.

Before you go

Know before you go

Timing & events

  • Peak season: Aug 1–7 is the busiest, priciest week on both coasts — book hotels, transfers, the boat and dinners well ahead.
  • Cinque Terre on a Monday (Aug 3) is busy but a touch calmer than the weekend; early train and afternoon ferry is the way.
  • Porto Cervo is at its glittering peak in early August — worth a dinner reservation everywhere.
  • Ferragosto (Aug 15) falls after your trip, so you miss the single most chaotic day.

Pacing for two

  • The arrival train is long after a redeye — Business/Executive seats and a relaxed boarding make it restful, not tiring.
  • Cinque Terre villages are steep — flat Monterosso plus Vernazza, by train and ferry, skip the trails.
  • Your Grand Hotel has a direct path to Cala Granu beach — no boat shuttle.
  • The La Maddalena boat day is seated and shaded.

A note on the fish allergy

  • Both coasts love seafood — flag it every time: "Sono allergica al pesce e ai frutti di mare." In Liguria, watch for anchovies (acciughe) hidden in sauces.
  • Liguria is easy: pesto (trofie/trenette), focaccia, farinata, pansoti with walnut sauce — all fish-free.
  • Sardinia too: porceddu (roast suckling pig), culurgiones, pane carasau, pecorino, and seadas for dessert.
  • Carry an allergy card in Italian and keep meds on hand, especially on boat days.

Booking notes

  • One round trip, one train, two island flights — far fewer moving parts than two separate fly-in legs.
  • Confirm the Tue Aug 4 Genoa → Olbia flight early.
  • Keep the Olbia → Rome feeder's buffer generous, since it's a separate ticket.
  • Reserve the boat, the ferry, Cinque Terre passes and dinners ahead.

Good to know

  • Italy uses Type F/L plugs, 230V — bring adapters.
  • Carry some cash; smaller spots can be card-shy.
  • No rental car needed — train, ferry and private transfers cover everything.
  • Validate regional train tickets before boarding where required.
